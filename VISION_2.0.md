# Sarathi — Project Constitution

**Artifact:** `VISION_2.0.md`  
**Status:** Foundational (non-versioned)  
**Nature:** Constitution — not a roadmap

This document defines enduring principles. It should outlive frameworks, models, interfaces, and programming languages. Roadmaps change; this almost never should.

It answers: *What kind of project is Sarathi?*  
It does not answer: *What are we building next?*

Contributors should read this before opening the codebase.

---

## 1. Why Sarathi Exists

Modern AI systems are excellent at retrieving information but poor at conveying wisdom with fidelity, context, and restraint. They optimize for fluency and coverage. They rarely optimize for whether guidance is grounded, proportionate, and honest about its limits.

Sarathi exists to explore whether **editorial governance** can bridge that gap: combining authentic source material, carefully curated interpretation, and a product experience designed to guide rather than overwhelm.

The first domain is the Bhagavad Gita—not because the architecture is inseparable from that text, but because a single, serious corpus is the right place to prove the method. Narrow focus is a strength. Depth before breadth.

Sarathi is therefore both a product and a research stance: that trust in knowledge systems can be engineered through separation of concerns, editorial discipline, progressive disclosure, and measurable evaluation—rather than claimed through branding or model scale alone.

---

## 2. Vision

In five to ten years, Sarathi aims to be remembered less for feature count than for standards.

- Become a **reference architecture** for editorially governed wisdom systems: immutable sources, curated intelligence, and a product layer that cannot quietly rewrite either.
- Demonstrate that **trust can be engineered**—through transparency, evaluation, and release discipline—not merely asserted.
- Show that AI interfaces can **prioritize understanding over verbosity**, and that restraint is a design virtue.
- Remain a project where **quality of guidance** is inspectable by developers and, where appropriate, visible to the public in engineering terms.

Expansion to other carefully curated domains is a strategic possibility, not a present obligation. Maturity of the first domain comes first.

---

## 3. Core Principles (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Authentic sources remain immutable.** Source text is not rewritten to suit the product, the model, or the moment.
2. **Editorial interpretation is always distinct from source material.** Users and systems must be able to tell scripture from explanation.
3. **Product experience must never compromise editorial integrity.** Convenience does not authorize bypass of review, approval, or grounding.
4. **More information is not always better.** Progressive disclosure is preferred to dumping context.
5. **Trust is earned through transparency and evaluation.** Claims about quality require measurable standards and honest limits.
6. **Simplicity is preferred over feature accumulation.** Capability without confidence is unfinished work.
7. **Safety and pastoral care are design constraints**, not post-hoc filters hoped for from generative fluency.
8. **Editorial and product evolve on separate tracks.** Content quality and application capability must not be conflated in versioning or release decisions.

---

## 4. Architectural Philosophy

Sarathi is organized as three layers that evolve independently:

```text
Authentic Knowledge
        ↓
Editorial Intelligence
        ↓
Product Experience
```

**Authentic Knowledge** holds primary sources. It is locked against casual change. Errata, if ever needed, are exceptional and deliberate. This layer answers: *What did the source say?*

**Editorial Intelligence** holds curated interpretation: families, enrichments, voice, misconceptions, and modern application written under an editorial guide. It answers: *How do we responsibly explain and apply this for seekers today?* Status is explicit (generated → reviewed → approved → locked). Approval is never automatic.

**Product Experience** holds conversation, journeys, progressive disclosure, evaluation, and interfaces. It answers: *How do we present guidance without overwhelming, misleading, or substituting for human judgment?* It consumes approved editorial material; it does not invent scripture or silently elevate unreviewed commentary to authority.

Independence of these layers is intentional. Product experiments must not destabilize the corpus. Editorial growth must not require reinventing the application. That separation is one of Sarathi’s defining ideas—and the reason the project is better described as a **knowledge governance platform** than as a retrieval chatbot with religious prompts.

---

## 5. What Sarathi Is Not

- **Not a replacement for human spiritual teachers**, community, or lived practice.
- **Not an authority on all philosophical or religious traditions.**
- **Not a mental health service**, crisis line, or clinical care provider.
- **Not an unrestricted language model** wrapped in spiritual framing.
- **Not a content-generation platform** for producing authoritative scripture or commentary on demand.
- **Not a engagement-maximizing consumer app** whose success is measured primarily by time spent.
- **Not a claim of final or exclusive interpretation** of the Gita.

These boundaries protect identity. Crossing them for growth would dissolve the project’s purpose.

---

## 6. Decision Framework

Every substantial proposal—feature, corpus expansion, partnership, or research direction—should answer four questions:

1. **Does it strengthen trust?**
2. **Does it preserve editorial integrity?**
3. **Does it simplify or complicate the user experience?**
4. **Would we still build this if user growth were irrelevant?**

If any answer is problematic, the proposal deserves extra scrutiny or rejection. Cleverness is not sufficient justification.

---

## 7. Success Metrics

Growth may follow from usefulness. It is not the primary definition of success.

Prefer outcomes such as:

- **Editorial quality** — fidelity, clarity, practicality, voice consistency, misconception handling
- **User trust** — whether people find answers grounded, proportionate, and honest
- **Conversation fitness** — whether first answers suffice; whether deeper levels are invited, not forced
- **Journey usefulness** — acceptance, completion, and abandonment with learning, not vanity
- **Transparency** — inspectable evaluation, known limitations, clear release separation
- **Reproducibility** — same inputs yield accountable retrieval and editorial paths
- **Benchmark performance** — gates that block releases when quality regresses

Vanity metrics (raw traffic, message volume, time-on-app) are secondary and may be misleading if they reward verbosity or dependency over understanding.

---

## 8. Things We Intentionally Reject

- Features that **bypass editorial review** or present unapproved material as settled guidance
- **AI-generated scripture or commentary** presented as authoritative
- Engagement mechanics designed to **maximize time spent** rather than value delivered
- Personalization that **obscures why** guidance was given
- Adding new domains **before the first domain is genuinely mature**
- Treating model upgrades as substitutes for **editorial discipline**
- Conflating **product releases** with **editorial approvals**
- Shipping capability that cannot be **evaluated** against stated principles

This section exists to prevent scope creep from well-intentioned curiosity.

---

## 9. Long-Term Research Questions

The project remains intellectually open. Prefer lasting questions over premature victory:

- Can editorial governance improve user trust in AI-mediated knowledge systems?
- How should wisdom-centered systems be evaluated—beyond retrieval accuracy and latency?
- What role should progressive disclosure play in explanations that aim to guide rather than overwhelm?
- Can transparent evaluation and release discipline become a standard pattern for knowledge systems?
- Where does curated journey design outperform open-ended generation—and where does it fail?
- How should systems communicate uncertainty and pastoral limits without abandoning usefulness?

Evidence from real use should refine these questions. The constitution does not declare them answered.

---

## Closing

Sarathi is a serious research and engineering effort: to govern knowledge carefully, present it with restraint, and make trust measurable.

When in doubt, prefer fidelity over fluency, clarity over coverage, and evidence over invention.

The next durable knowledge about this project should come from people who use it—not from further elaboration of intent in documents like this one.
